Everything in here is "literal" i.e. it doesn't need any template processing.

types.h and assert.h are general stuff. 
The capitalised things are specific structs used by templates. 
The lower-case directories are modules.

Modules usually have a public header called h.h and included as "module/h.h"
They might have more headers like struct.h which is kinda private to the module.
There may be any number of .c files and they'll all be glued together into
  gen/module.o

All the module.o files from lit or tpt get joined into gen/o.o  
test and hive dump their object files under bin instead of gen so
  they don't end up in gen/o.o
